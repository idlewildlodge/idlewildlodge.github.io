Idlewild was named by Ella Van Bramer in 1906. Here she established a homestead and tourist retreat, and soon after began construction on Idlewild’s first cabins.

Coykendall Homestead Carroll Coykendall began homesteading here in 1904. He would receive a patent for eighty acres adjacent to the Idlewild homestead in 1909. Turkington Cabin A one room cabin owned by Edward Turkington, a pharmacist from Loveland. More Information Stewart Cabin A cabin built in 1906 and owned by a Mr. Stewart from Loveland. Would later become the foundation structure of Idlewild Lodge. More Information Loveland-Estes Park Stage Line From 1905-1906 the Johnson Brothers ran a stage line up the Big Thompson Canyon. Idlewild served as a relay station where horses could rest and where guests could enjoy a hot meal. More Information Riley Road The Riley Road was completed in 1904. It was the first road through the canyon that went through the "narrows", which significantly shortened the distance and travel time from Loveland to Estes Park. Idlewild Resort Ella Van Bramer built a camping resort with at least nine fancy tent sites. Permanent tents on raised wooden platforms were rented by tourists, fisherman, and mountaineers during the summer months. More Information Idlewild Milk House Ella Van Bramer built a milkhouse over Idlewild creek to store food for her guests. She also likely stored butter here, which she was producing commercially. More Information Idle Wild House The first cabin built at Idlewild. Served as Ella Van Bramer's personal residence and headquarters for her Idlewild Camping Resort. More Information
